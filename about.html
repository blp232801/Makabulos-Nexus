About Makabulos Memorial High School

Our History

Makabulos Memorial High School, established in 1939, stands as one of the enduring educational institutions in Victoria, Tarlac. Located along Rizal Street, the school is recognized by the Department of Education (DepEd) and continues to provide quality secondary education under the K–12 curriculum. Serving students from Grades 7 to 12, the school prepares learners for higher studies, technical careers, or immediate employment, while instilling values that shape responsible and patriotic citizens.

The school carries the proud name of General Francisco Makabulos, a revolutionary hero and son of Tarlac. General Makabulos played a crucial role during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. Known for his courage, vision, and dedication to freedom, he established a provisional revolutionary government in Central Luzon, demonstrating his leadership and deep commitment to the cause of independence. By naming the institution in his honor, the school ensures that his legacy of patriotism, bravery, and nation-building lives on in every generation of students.

From its early beginnings in 1939 under its first director, Sinforoso Pascual, Makabulos Memorial High School has grown alongside the community of Victoria. Over the decades, it has adapted to the changing needs of the nation’s education system, most notably embracing the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013 (K–12). Today, the school offers both Junior High School and Senior High School programs, with specialized strands such as STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) and HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences) to equip students for diverse futures.

Guided by the mission of providing holistic education, the school blends academic excellence with character formation and life skills. Inspired by General Makabulos’s example, it emphasizes discipline, courage, responsibility, and service to the community—values that reflect the revolutionary spirit of its namesake. Under the leadership of Principal Jaime Pascual, the dedicated faculty continues to nurture students in an environment that encourages both intellectual growth and moral integrity.

Situated in the heart of Victoria and close to landmarks such as the Victoria Public Auditorium and Iglesia ni Cristo, the school is a familiar and central institution in the town. For more than eight decades, Makabulos Memorial High School has stood not only as a center of learning but also as a living tribute to General Francisco Makabulos, ensuring that his heroism continues to inspire young Filipinos to dream, learn, and serve their nation.
